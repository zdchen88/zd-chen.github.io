Dr. Chen received the B. Eng. degree in Radio Engineering from Fuzhou University, Fujian, P.R. of China, the M. of Appl. Sc. degree in Radio Engineering from Nanjing Institute of Technology (currently Southeast University), Nanjing, P.R. of China, the PhD degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of Ottawa, Ottawa, Canada, respectively. He was a winner of the Association of Professors University of Ottawa Student Award (1990), Ontario Graduate Student Scholarship (1991-1992) and the Natural Science and Engineering Research Council of Canada (NSERC) Postdoctoral Fellowship (1993).

From January of 1993 to August of 1993, he was with Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, McGill University, Montreal, Quebec, as a NSERC postdoctoral fellow under the supervision of Professor Peter Silvester. Since September of 1993, he has been on staff with the Faculty of Engineering (formerly the Technical University of Nova Scotia), Dalhousie University, Halifax, Canada, where he is presently a professor.

Dr. Chen has been active in both teaching and research. Right since his first term with Dalhousie University, he has been teaching various undergraduate and graduate courses in the areas of RF/Microwave communication electronics/systems, antennas and electromagnetics (5 courses per year on average), and has maintained good evaluations. He has authored or co-authored over 100 journal/conference papers and industrial reports in the areas of RF/microwave circuit and system design and computational electromagnetics. He was one of the key originators in developing new numerical algorithms a recent scheme, the so-called ADI-FDTD method. He was also involved in designing a new class of compact RF front-end circuits for wireless communications. He was a sole/principal investigator of more than 10 grants from both government and industry, including the recent Atlantic Innovation Fund on smart transceiver (2002-), Canadian NSERC Strategic Grant on novel RF front-end module (1999-), grants from Nortel Networks Ltd. on mm-wave filters (1998-2001) and grants from Orion Electronics Ltd. on low-profile antennas and DSP schemes for direction finding (1998-2001). He has obtained almost three million dollars of research funding. He has completed supervision of 5 postdoctoral fellows, 1 PhD student, 7 M.A.Sc. students and more than 40 undergraduate students for their senior projects.

Dr. Chen served as an editorial board member for the IEEE Transactions on Microwave Theory and Techniques. He is also an invited reviewer for various international journals and research grant agencies. He was an invited speaker, session chair and organizer for a number of international conferences, workshops and seminars. He is a senior member of IEEE, a registered professional engineer and has served as a consultant for a number of local companies.

Dr. Chen has also been involved in various professional and university administrative activities. He served as the ECE graduate advisor (2002- and 1998-1999), the Acting ECE Co-op advisor (2001-2002), a University Senate Library committee member (2001-2003), a University Scholarship Committee (2001-2002), a University Instructional Development Committee member (1998-1999) and the ECE staff, faculty and head search committee member (1998). He was the Chair of IEEE Atlantic Section and a member of Board of Directors of IEEE Canada (2000-2001). He was also the Chairman of the Board of Director for Halifax Chinese Language School (2002-).

Dr. Chen's general research area is in Computational Electromagnetics and RF/Microwave Engineering for communications and microelectronics. His current teaching and research involvements are in the area computational electromagnetics and wireless communications that include intelligent RF/microwave transceiving systems, smart antennas, high-frequency electronics, and generic electromagnetic time-domain modeling. He will continue to be involved in various university service and administrative functions and professional and volunteer activities.
