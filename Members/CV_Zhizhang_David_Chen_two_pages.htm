Brief Biography of Dr. Zhizhang (David) Chen

Dr. Chen received a B.Eng. degree in Radio Engineering from Fuzhou University, Fujian, China, in 1982, a M.A.Sc. degree in Radio Engineering from Southeast University (formerly Nanjing Institute of Technology), Nanjing, China, in 1986 and a Ph.D. degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of Ottawa, Ottawa, Ontario, Canada, in 1992, respectively. He was the recipient of the Association of Professors University of Ottawa Student Award (1990), the Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada (NSERC) Postdoctoral Fellowship (1993), and a Chinese National Natural Science Fund for Distinguished Young Scholars (oversea; 2006-2008). He was also the recipient of the Association of Professional Engineers of Nova Scotia (APENS) 2005 Engineering Award, a 2006 Dalhousie University graduate teaching excellence award, the 2006 Professor of the Year in Electrical and Computer Engineering of Dalhousie University, the 2013 IEEE Canada Fessenden Medal and the 2007, 2016 Dalhousie University Faculty of Engineering Senior Faculty Research Award (twice) and 2017 Dalhousie University Research Professorship. He is the Fellow of the Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineers (IEEE), the Fellow of the Canadian Academy of Engineering (CAE), the Fellow of Engineering Institute of Canada (EIC) and a registered professional engineer and has served as a consultant for local companies.

 

From January to August 1993, Dr. Chen was a NSERC postdoctoral fellow with the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, McGill University, Montreal, Quebec, Canada. Since September of 1993, he has been with the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Dalhousie University, Halifax, Nova Scotia, where he is now a full professor and the Head of the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering. He has also been a visiting or adjunct professor with the University of Nottingham (United Kingdom), École Nationale Supérieure des Télécommunications de Bretagne (France), Hong Kong University Science and Technology, Shanghai Jiaotong University (China), Beihang University (China), Fuzhou University and the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China. He has been appointed as a Killam Chair Professor in Wireless Technology at Dalhousie University (2005-2010). He has also been appointed as a member of NSERC (Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada) Strategic Project Selection Panel C Information and Communication Technology (2004-2007) and the Discovery Grant Evaluation Committee for Electrical and Computer Engineering (2008-2011).

 

Dr. Chen has been active in teaching, research and professional services. He has been teaching various undergraduate and graduate courses in the areas of RF/Microwave communication electronics/systems, antennas and electromagnetics, and has maintained good student evaluations. He has completed supervision/co-supervision of postdoctoral fellows, PhD students, Master students, research engineers/associates, and undergraduate students for their senior projects. Four of the senior student-projects were awarded a national IEEE prize and IEEE regional prizes. He has authored or co-authored over 310 refereed journal/conference papers and 24 industrial reports, published two books, contributed to 2 books, edited 1 research monograph and 1 conference proceeding and, and filed 6 patents. He was one of the key originators in developing new numerical algorithms (including the highly cited and commercialized unconditionally stable three-dimensional finite-difference time-domain method) and in designing a new class of compact RF circuits and systems for wireless communications and wireless power transfer. He has obtained more than four million dollars of research funding, and has been a sole/principal investigator of more than twenty-eight grants from both government and industry; they include NSERC Discovery Grants since 1993, a NSERC Discovery Accelerator Supplement Grant (2012-2015), two NSERC Strategic Project Grants: one on novel RF front-end module (1999-2003) and another on Ultra-wideband Impulse Radios (2010-2014); an research & development contract from Martec Ltd. in developing structure composite microwave materials for radar applications (2011-2013); an Atlantic Innovation Fund on generic smart RF transceivers (2003-2007); Nortel Networks Ltd. research grants on mm-wave filters (1998-2001) and Orion Electronics Ltd. research contract on low-profile antennas and DSP for direction finding (1998-2001).

 

Dr. Chen has been an invited speaker, session chair, program member, organizer and conference chair for many international conferences, workshops and seminars (including IEEE Conference on Emerging Technologies, IEEE International Microwave Symposium). He has served as guest editors and an editorial board member for the IEEE Transactions on Microwave Theory and Techniques, IEEE Microwave Magazine, International Journal of Numerical Modeling (by John Wiley) and Journal of Wireless Power Transfer (by Cambridge). He is currently a founding associate editor of the newly started IEEE Journal of Multiscale and Multiphysics Computational Techniques. He is also a reviewer for various international journals and research grant agencies. He is a member of Microwave Field Theory Committee, Wireless Communication Committee and Wireless Energy Transfer and Conversion Committee of IEEE Microwave Theory and Techniques Society, and its Speaker’s Bureau member. He was also the founding Chair of the joint Signal Processing and Microwave Theory & Techniques Chapter of IEEE Atlantic Canada (2009-2011), the Chair of IEEE Canada Atlantic Section and a member of the Board of Directors for IEEE Canada (2000-2001).

 

Dr. Chen has also been involved in various university administrative/service activities, such as, the Head of the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering of Dalhousie University (2011-present); The Review Committee for the Head of Dalhousie Mechanical Engineering (2017); Dalhousie Senate Academic Programs and Research Committee; Dalhousie University Senator (2010-2013); the Engineering Deanship Review Committee (2009); the Advisory Committee for Dalhousie Institute for Research in Materials (2006-2008); Dalhousie Engineering Faculty Tenure and Promotion Committee (2003-2006; Chair for 2006); Dalhousie Librarian Review committee (2003-2004); Dalhousie engineering faculty Associate Dean Search Committee (2003); ECE graduate coordinator and member of Dalhousie graduate faculty council (2003-2005 & 1998-1999); acting ECE co-op advisor (2001-2002); Dalhousie Senate Library Committee (2001-2003); Dalhousie Scholarship Committee (2001-2002); Dalhousie Instructional Development Committee (1998-1999) and ECE staff, faculty and head search committees (1998-2011). Dr. Chen has also been active in local communities. He has served as the President of Chinese Society of Nova Scotia (2006-2007) and the Chairman of Halifax Chinese Language School Board of Directors (2003).

 

Dr. Chen's general research area is in Computational Electromagnetics, RF/Microwave Engineering and Wireless Technology and Applications.
