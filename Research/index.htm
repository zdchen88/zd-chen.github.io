Objective and overview

The general objectives of this research group is

(1) to develop new computational electromagnetic modelling methods (e.g., unconditionally stable techniques, unification of numerical emthods),
(2) to design innovative RF/microwave circuits/systems and antennas (e.g., wireless sensing and power transfer systems),
(3) to research and investigate new electromagnetic phenomena (e.g., bioelectricity and bioelectromagnetics), and
(4) to develop new electromagnetic materials and applications (e.g., metasurfances, new engineered materials)

More specifically, we are interested in projects and topics related to bioelectricity, bioelectromagnetics, novel electromagnetic or optical materials/structures, analysis of digital and high-frequency signal integrity, and design of intelligent RF/microwave radio systems, smart antennas, ultra-wide-band technology, high-frequency electronics and wireless power transfer and harveting technology.

The laboratory has been successful in attracting a talented group of graduate students and in interacting with talented colleagues. Membership in the laboratory is treated as a privilege and members are expected to exhibit a high degree of self-motivation, independence, and creativity. The work in the laboratory contributes actively to the advancement of both teaching and research in its areas of expertise, as can be evidenced from its publications

At the moment, we has several active research projects supported by the Natural Science and Engineering Research Council of Canada and the corporate sector. Although fundamental research is still part of our research activities (as it renders us with long-term vitality), we are actively seeking more and larger contract research projects from industry in the coming years. These opportunities will allow us to continually contribute to the knowledge base and to aid to the healthy development of our economy and technology.
