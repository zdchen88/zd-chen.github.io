RF/Microwave System Design for Telecommunication

(Outline)

 

Instructor: Dr. Z. Chen

January-April, 2011

 

Objectives:

This course provides essential practical design techniques for radio systems that operate at frequencies 3MHz to 100GHz and will be employed in the telecommunication service. The contents will cover mainly on various RF/microwave links.

 

Topics:

  • Fundamental Parameters of Antennas
  • Radio propagation Characteristics
  • Microwave Radiolinks
  • Satellite Communications
  • Mobile Communications
  • Radio terminal Design Considerations
  • Other Communications Related Technologies

 

Textbook:

Roger L. Freeman, "Radio System Design for Telecommunications", John Wiley & Sons, Latest version

 

Other Reference Books

  • Books on antennas
  • Books on radio propagation channels and communication systems
  • Some handouts

 

Marking Scheme

  • One written test (open book, 2 or 3 hours) at the end of the term
  • assignments or course projects

 

Final grade = 50% Assignments (or course projects) + 50% Test

 

Course Organization

  1. Lectures: The chapters from the textbook will be designated and posted on the web for you to self-study in conjunction with assignments.  The class will then gather every week. The gathering is mainly for discussions of problems in the assignments and reading materials. The instructor may give a very limited number of condensed lectures on the chapters of the textbook that are to be covered, pending on the progress of the class.
  2. Assignments or Class Projects: there will be one assignment or class project after each designated chapter normally. The deadlines of submissions will be posted on the web site.
