Graduate Program

(General Information)

The Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering (ECE) offers the following post-graduate degrees:  

Master of Engineering (M. Eng.) Degree (project based) requires 7 course credits plus a seminar. A project is required (1 or 2 credits out of the required 10). The normal time a student should take to complete this degree is between 12 and 18 months.

Master of Applied Science (M. A. Sc.) Degree (research or thesis based) requires 4 courses plus seminar and a research thesis. The normal time to complete this program is between 18 and 24 months. The Master of Applied Science degree is normally one of the requirements for admission to the Doctor of Philosophy degree program.

Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D) Degree requires 4 courses plus seminar if a candidate already has a research Master's degree, otherwise it requires 12 courses. An original research thesis is also required. It usually takes from three to four years to finish.

 

ECE faculty members are currently engaged in a number of research areas. For more information, please go to the ECE Faculty Home Page.

Dalhousie Faculty of Graduate Studies and Faculty of Engineering.

 

Admission Requirements and Application Process

In order to be admitted to an academic program, an applicant must meet the admission requirements set up by the Dalhousie Faculty of Graduate Studies and the Faculty of Engineering. In addition to these, the ECE department has more specific requirements as described below.

 

For admission to the Master’s Degree Program:

Possession of a baccalaureate degree or its academic equivalent from an academic institution recognized by Dalhousie, in which the language of instruction is English, or

Four years of full-time post-secondary instruction at a university where English is the primary language of instruction and in a country where English is the principal language, or

Possession of a baccalaureate degree or its academic equivalent from an academic institution recognized by Dalhousie with a satisfactory score on an approved English language examination such as TOEFL (Test of English as Foreign Language). The Departmental requirement for TOEFL is 580 (Paper) or 237 (Computer).

 

For admission to the Doctoral Degree Program:

Possession of a research Master’s degree or its academic equivalent from an academic institution recognized by Dalhousie, in which the language of instruction is English, or

Possession of a first-class non-thesis Master’s Degree, in a country where English is the principal language, may be considered for admission to the PhD program where evidence of independent research can be clearly demonstrated, such as a major research paper, presentation at scholarly conferences, publications in journals, etc. Such candidates may also be admitted into a Master’s program with the possibility of transferring to the doctoral program, or

Possession of a research Master’s degree or its academic equivalent from an academic institution recognized by Dalhousie with a satisfactory score on an approved English language examination such as TOEFL. The Departmental requirement for TOEFL is 580 (Paper) or 237 (Computer).

 

Foreign students may be required to enter the M.A.Sc. program as a qualifying period prior to consideration for the Ph.D. program.

 

In order to be admitted to an academic graduate program, an applicant must be accepted by a professor into his/her research program, in addition to meeting all the above requirements for admission. For information regarding research areas of ECE faculty members, please go to the ECE Faculty Home Page .

 

 

Limited financial aid in the form of scholarships, research and teaching assistantships may be available, depending on (1) an applicant’s request in his/her application, (2) excellence of the applicant and (3) availability of funds. Applicants are encouraged to explore the possibility of obtaining financial support or scholarships from other sources such as NSERC scholarships (www.nserc.ca) and Killam scholarships (http://www.dalgrad.dal.ca/killam/), or personal funds.

 

The application fee is $70.00 and can be paid by money order or any major credit card.  U. S. dollars are accepted at the current exchange rate.

Useful Information for New and Current Graduate Students in the ECE Graduate Programs

You are strongly encouraged to visit Dalhousie Faculty of Graduate Studies site at www.dalgrad.dal.ca and Faculty of Engineering site at http://www.dal.ca/~engiwww on various issues related to your graduate studies. The issues specific to your ECE graduate program are described below.

Financial Aid and Scholarships:

Limited financial aid is provided in the form of graduate scholarships, teaching and research assistantships, subject to availability of funds. Scholarships are normally offered and administrated by various sponsoring organizations such as NSERC and University. If you are interested, you need to either find out information yourself or pay attention to messages from the university, Faculty of Engineering and ECE department. Teaching assistantships are administrated and assigned by the ECE department based on a graduate student’s need and his/her experience in teaching, including English proficiency. A graduate student who receives a teaching assistantship performs teaching assistant duties for an undergraduate class and is under the supervision of the class instructor. Research assistantships normally come from professors’ research grants and are administrated by individual professors. They are usually awarded to a professor’s student by the professor. The student who receives a research assistantship is expected to conduct research directed by the professor.  

Computer Accounts:

Applications for computer accounts are available from the Academic Computing Help Desk, located behind Student Services in 'B' building of Sexton Campus. You must fill out the application in full and present it with a valid ID card in order to activate your account. Once this is done you can use the computer labs on campus. Please be sure to notify the office of your e-mail address so you don't miss any important notices.

 

Graduate Advisor:

Dr. Zhizhang (David) Chen is the current Graduate Faculty Advisor. Please contact him by email at z.chen@dal.ca or by phone at (902) 494-6042 if you have any questions or concerns regarding your studies.

 

Identification Card:

You are required to obtain an ID card, which states your name, status, and student number. This can be obtained at the Student Services Centre, Building B (just inside the main entrance doors), Sexton Campus.

 

Mail:

Personal mail should NOT be forwarded to the University. University mail will be placed in mail slots outside the office. You name will appear on the slot with your Supervisor's name. You will be notified by e-mail of any faxes or confidential mailings, which you can pick up in the office.

 

Notice Boards:

General notices, seminars, thesis presentations, call for papers, employment opportunities, graduate course information, and University posters are posted on the various message boards around the department. You should check these boards - especially the Graduate notice board, at least once a week.

 

Office Space:

If you are registered full time and require an office in which to study, please inform the ECE office (C367) and a desk in one of the graduate rooms will be assigned to you. Keys to these rooms are available, but you may have a few days wait after your request has been submitted. There is a $20.00 deposit per key, required at time of pick up. This will be refunded when the key is returned.

 

Pay:

Paycheques are available from the ECE office on a biweekly basis for students working as a teaching assistant.  Paycheques for a research assistant are available on the 27th of each month (or the Friday before if the 27th falls on the weekend). You must sign for your cheques when you pick them up from the ECE office.

 

Phone:

There is one phone in each Graduate room. These phones are to be used for local calls only. We have a Voice Mail system called Message Manager.  Please ask the other Graduate students (or see office staff) to learn how to get your messages. The password to enable the system can be obtained from the office secretary. Please do NOT change the password!

 

Photocopier:

There is a photocopier across the hall from the ECE office. If your supervisor deems it necessary, you will be given an access code enabling you to make copies on his/her research account. Please be advised you CANNOT copy books, as this constitutes a copyright infringement. Also, remember to be quick, as many others use this machine and you may have to give priority to Department staff and faculty members.

 

Printer:

There is also a printer in the photocopy room across from the ECE office. The printer is designated for use mainly by staff, faculty members and instructors. Some faculty labs are able to print to this machine. However, you need to check with the ECE office to see if you can use the printer. Even you have the permission to use the printer, do not print large documents at peak times. Be responsible in your printing!

 

Registration and Student Accounts:

These are handled by the Student Services Centre. Their hours are 10am - 4pm, Monday to Friday. Payments can be made by cheque, cash, credit card, and Interac.

 

Residence:

If you are looking for a place to stay while at Dalhousie, you may contact Housing and Conference Services. They can be reached at 494-3365 between 8:30 and 4:30pm, or via e-mail at Housing@dal.ca.

 

Student Societies:

There are a number of student societies that may help you on various issues you encounter on campus. Two of these are Graduate Student Society and International Student Services.
