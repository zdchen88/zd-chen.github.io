ECED4460:
COMMUNICATION ELECTRONICS

COURSE OUTLINE

Objectives:

  • The student will become familiar with basic instrumentation and components used in RF/microwave communications electronics.
  • The student will learn basic techniques for designing RF/Microwave amplifiers, oscillators, and mixers using either distributive or lumped circuit elements.
  • The student will learn the concepts of computer optimization through a computer aided design project.
  • The student will gain hands-on experience, intuition, and confidence in designing, testing and debugging RF and microwave circuits

Lecture Topics (in the forms of classroom and online teaching):

  1. Transmission line theory and representation of a RF/Microwave network
  2. Noises and Matching networks
  3. Amplifier Design (stability circles, constant gain circles, constant NF circles)
  4. Couplers/combiners and baluns
  5. Oscillators (phase locked loops) (if time permits)
  6. Mixers/detectors (if time allows)
  7. Introduction to optimization and CAD (Lectures/Labs)

Project:

In groups of maximum three persons.

  1. Low noise RF/microwave amplifier design

References/Textbook:

  • Class handouts
  • Lecture notes
  • R. Ludwig and P.Bretchko, "RF Circuit Design-Theory and Application", Prentice Hall Inc., lastest edition

Marking Scheme:

  • 1 written test at the end of the term; open book
  • 3-4 assignments
  • 1 lab project (due on the date of the written test. You will need to ask your TA to inspect your experiment set-up after completing your project)
    Assignments: 20%
    Written test: 20%
    Project (design, fabrication, testing and written presentation): 60%
